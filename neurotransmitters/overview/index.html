    Neurotransmitters — Overview¶

    What Are Neurotransmitters?¶

    Neurotransmitters are endogenous chemical messengers synthesised and released by neurons (and some glial cells) to transmit signals across synapses to target cells. They govern virtually every physiological and psychological function: mood, cognition, sleep, appetite, pain, motor control, and autonomic regulation.

    Key insight — Most neurotransmitters are derived from common dietary amino acids. Their synthesis, release, and degradation are tightly regulated by enzymes that critically depend on vitamins, minerals, and cofactors. Nutritional status directly sets the ceiling for neurotransmitter output.

    Classification of Neurotransmitters¶

    Class Examples Primary Precursor
    Monoamines — Catecholamines Dopamine, Norepinephrine, Epinephrine L-Tyrosine
    Monoamines — Indolamines Serotonin, Melatonin L-Tryptophan
    Monoamines — Imidazolamines Histamine L-Histidine
    Amino Acids (inhibitory) GABA, Glycine Glutamate, Serine
    Amino Acids (excitatory) Glutamate, Aspartate Glutamine, α-ketoglutarate
    Cholinergic Acetylcholine Choline + Acetyl-CoA
    Opioid Peptides Endorphins, Enkephalins, Dynorphins POMC, Proenkephalin
    Neuropeptides Substance P, NPY, CRH, Oxytocin Varied protein precursors
    Gaseous Nitric Oxide (NO), CO, H₂S Arginine, Haem, Cysteine

    The Synthesis–Release–Degradation Cycle¶

    All neurotransmitters share a common functional lifecycle:

    Dietary Precursor (amino acid / choline / etc.)
        │
        ▼ [Rate-limiting synthesis step — enzyme + cofactors]
Neurotransmitter (stored in vesicles)
        │
        ▼ [Action potential → vesicle fusion → exocytosis]
Synapse (binds post-synaptic receptors)
        │
        ├─► Reuptake (transporter proteins — SERT, DAT, NET, GAT)
        │          → recycled or degraded in presynaptic terminal
        │
        └─► Enzymatic Degradation
                   → MAO-A, MAO-B, COMT, AChE, GABA-T
                   → inactive metabolites → urine / faeces

    Rate-Limiting Enzymes¶

    Neurotransmitter Rate-Limiting Enzyme Key Cofactors
    Serotonin Tryptophan hydroxylase (TPH1/2) Tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4), Fe²⁺, O₂
    Dopamine Tyrosine hydroxylase (TH) BH4, Fe²⁺, O₂
    Norepinephrine Dopamine-β-hydroxylase (DBH) Vitamin C, Cu²⁺, O₂
    GABA Glutamic acid decarboxylase (GAD) Pyridoxal-5′-phosphate (PLP / B6)
    Acetylcholine Choline acetyltransferase (ChAT) Acetyl-CoA (B5/pantothenate chain)
    Histamine Histidine decarboxylase (HDC) Pyridoxal-5′-phosphate (PLP / B6)
    Melatonin Acetylserotonin methyltransferase (ASMT) SAM (methylation — requires B12, folate, B2)

    Shared Cofactors and Nutrient Dependencies¶

    Nutrient Role in Neurotransmitter Metabolism
    Vitamin B6 (PLP) Essential cofactor for DOPA decarboxylase (serotonin, dopamine, GABA, histamine synthesis)
    Vitamin B9 (Folate / 5-MTHF) Regenerates BH4; provides methyl groups for catecholamine methylation via SAM
    Vitamin B12 (methylcobalamin) SAM regeneration; methionine synthase; myelin maintenance
    Vitamin B3 (Niacin) Tryptophan competes with NAD⁺ synthesis — deficiency diverts tryptophan away from serotonin
    Vitamin B5 (Pantothenate) Acetyl-CoA synthesis for acetylcholine production
    Vitamin C (Ascorbate) DBH cofactor (norepinephrine synthesis); antioxidant protection of BH4
    Vitamin D Regulates tyrosine hydroxylase and tryptophan hydroxylase gene expression
    Iron (Fe²⁺) TH and TPH enzyme cofactor; also required for BH4 recycling
    Zinc Co-factor for DOPA decarboxylase; modulates NMDA receptor; required for MAO activity
    Magnesium NMDA receptor blocker (prevents excitotoxicity); cofactor for many kinases in signalling
    Copper (Cu²⁺) DBH cofactor (dopamine → norepinephrine)
    Tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) Essential cofactor for TH (dopamine) and TPH (serotonin); regenerated by folate/B12
    SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) Methyl donor for COMT (catecholamine inactivation), HNMT (histamine), ASMT (melatonin)
    Omega-3 fatty acids (DHA) Modulate receptor density (5-HT, D2, NMDA); membrane fluidity for synaptic vesicle release

    Degradation Pathways Summary¶

    Enzyme Substrates Products Inhibited By
    MAO-A Serotonin, Norepinephrine, Dopamine 5-HIAA, MHPG, HVA Moclobemide, phenelzine, some SSRIs
    MAO-B Dopamine, Phenylethylamine, Histamine HVA, imidazole acetaldehyde Selegiline, rasagiline; reversibly by curcumin
    COMT Dopamine, Norepinephrine, Epinephrine, catechol-oestrogens 3-MT, normetanephrine, metanephrine Entacapone, tolcapone (Parkinson's drugs)
    AChE Acetylcholine Choline + Acetate Donepezil, galantamine (Alzheimer's drugs)
    GABA-T GABA Succinic semialdehyde Vigabatrin (anticonvulsant)
    HNMT Histamine (CNS) N-methylhistamine SKF 91488 (research compound)
    DAO (diamine oxidase) Histamine (gut/plasma) Imidazole acetaldehyde Alcohol, metformin; B6/copper deficiency
